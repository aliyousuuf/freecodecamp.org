Roti or Ruti or Chapati: An easy recipe that you ever find

Ruti, also known as chapati, is a round, flat bread commonly eaten in South Asia, especially in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. It’s made from simple ingredients like whole wheat flour, water, and sometimes salt, cooked on a hot griddle. This staple food is soft, versatile, and pairs well with curries, vegetables, or lentils.This page will describe an easy way to make ruti or chapati.

Ingredients

  • Whole Wheat Flour (Atta)
  • Water
  • Salt (Optional)
  • Oil or Ghee (Optional)
  • Tools
    • Mixing Bowl
    • Rolling Pin (Belan)
    • Flat Rolling Board (Chakla) or Clean Surface
    • Tawa or Flat Griddle
    • Tongs or Spatula
    • Clean Cloth or Kitchen Towel (Optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix 2 cups whole wheat flour with a pinch of salt.
  2. Gradually add ¾–1 cup lukewarm water
  3. Knead into a smooth, soft dough.
  4. Cover and rest dough for 15–30 minutes.
  5. Divide dough into 8–10 equal balls.
  6. Roll each ball into a thin, round circle.
  7. Heat a tawa or griddle on medium flame.
  8. Place ruti on tawa, cook until bubbles form.
  9. Flip and cook other side, pressing lightly.
  10. Brush with ghee (optional) and serve hot.
